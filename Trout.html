Trout

Trout fishing is all about matching what they’re eating and keeping things light and natural. In rivers and streams, a small #10–#18 gold-ribbed hare’s ear nymph or a classic Adams parachute dry fly in size 12–16 will catch fish almost anywhere in the country. For stocked or lake trout, a simple 1/16–1/4 oz Kastmaster spoon or a small Rooster Tail spinner in silver, gold, or black works stupidly well—just cast and reel steadily. If you’re fishing clear water, go with 4–6 lb fluorocarbon line so they don’t spook. My go-to cheap combo that still catches tons of trout is a $7–$10 pack of Panther Martin spinners (size 2 or 4) and a $12–$18 ultralight spinning rod—you can literally feel every head shake. Trout aren’t leader-shy like bass, so you don’t need anything fancy—just small, shiny, or buggy stuff that looks alive.
